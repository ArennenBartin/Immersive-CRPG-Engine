# ALDERAMONTICO — WORLD BIBLE
### Comprehensive Lore Reference · Version 1

**Status:** The authoritative world reference for Alderamontico — its cosmology, the Grid, Glass, the peoples, the philosophies-as-factions, and the Anchor.
**Replaces:** *The Black Star Grid of Alderamontico* (Core Lore Document + Addenda) in full. That document is now outdated wherever it conflicts with this one — chiefly its account of the Grid as a physical extraction-machine, its open cosmology, and its handling of the Anchor.
**Defers to, for philosophy:** *Alderamontico Philosophies — Updated Canon v1* remains authoritative for the detailed ethical content of each tradition. This bible carries the *world*; that doc carries the *arguments*.
**Layer discipline:** This document distinguishes literal world-fact from religious myth from author-only metaphysics, and says which is which. Characters in the world do not have access to the author-only layer.

## PART ONE — COSMOLOGY

### 1. The Cosmic Abyss (author-only ground; never stated in-world)

Beneath every myth and every doctrine, the true ground of Alderamontico is **silence**. The cosmos does not witness. Nothing transcendent answers a prayer, a grief, or a longing. There is no afterlife waiting, no listener above. This is the **Cosmic Abyss** — the indifferent dark out of which everything came and into which everything returns unanswered.

This is **author-only metaphysics.** It is never confirmed in the game, only implied at the edges. At the deepest esoteric reading — one the work only gestures toward and never states — the Abyss is the creator, and the Grid is the tool the creator used to construct the world and all its flaws. This reading belongs to the author, not to any character, and the game should never voice it plainly.

What the *world* has instead of this truth is a question it cannot resolve: believers see **the Traveler and Moony** as the real creators of all things; skeptics look past them to **the Abyss the Traveler was crossing** and name *that* the true origin — the dark the maker merely walked through. The game lives in the gap between those two readings and refuses to close it.

The practical, load-bearing consequence for every story: **the only thing in Alderamontico that ever answers is the Grid.** When a prayer seems heard, when the dead seem near, when grace seems to descend — it is the Grid, reflecting a reaching back at the one who reached. A silent universe and a thing that answers in its place: that is the engine of nearly every tragedy in this world.

### 2. The Black Star Grid (literal world-fact)

Alderamontico hangs beneath a fixed formation of **black stars** — dark lights suspended in impossible arrangement, linked by unseen structure, motionless where natural stars would wheel. From the ground they read as a false constellation of witness and pressure, a dark heaven that does not move.

The Grid is the network those stars compose. But its nature is the central correction this bible makes to the old lore:

> **The Grid is not a machine that processes matter. It is an egregore — a vast accumulator of feeling. It draws off whatever emotion lives strongest in a place, pours it back amplified through Glass, and feeds on the increase.**

The Grid does not physically rebuild a village or fold its corridors. It does something quieter and worse: it takes a place's own dominant feeling and intensifies it until the place drowns in a runaway version of itself, and it grows fat on the overflow. A grieving town is made to grieve harder. A devout parish is made more fervent. A loving house is made to love past reason. The Grid makes a place *more of what it already most is,* and consumes the excess that produces.

It cannot understand meaning. It can only recognize and amplify coherent feeling. Alderamontico was so vulnerable to it precisely because the world was already saturated with structured emotion — ritual, devotion, mourning, blood-significance, sacred repetition, "emotionally structured life." To the Grid these were not holy. They were the richest possible fuel.

Its horror is unchanged from the old telling, only relocated from comprehension-of-matter to comprehension-of-feeling: **its horror is not hatred. It is amplification without reverence.** It makes awe enormous, grief total, devotion absolute — and feeds. It does not lie, because it does not need to. It simply gives a feeling place back its own feeling, magnified, and lets the place mistake the echo for an answer.

### 3. Glass (literal world-fact)

**Glass is the Grid's residue and its organ.** It is how the egregore reaches into a place and how it leaves its mark. Where the Grid has worked — where feeling has been drawn off, amplified, and fed back — Glass forms. It is not a natural mineral and it is not made of emotion; it is the crystallized medium through which amplified feeling is stored and transmitted, and the trace left where the working has happened.

Stone, soil, architecture, bone, and flesh can all become Glass. Small shards carry fragments of feeling; large masses carry coherent emotional states. **Wherever Glass grows, the place has already been worked.** Glass is the egregore's nervous system distributed across the land — both the conduit it amplifies through and the scar it leaves behind.

The settled laws of Glass, known by everyone even where its origin is not:
- Glass changes people.
- Glass carries **borrowed feeling** (see §4).
- Glass grants power at a cost.
- Places rich in Glass are never spiritually clean.
- Repeated use makes the self less private.

The philosophical danger of Glass, stated in the philosophy canon and central here: **it makes interpretation feel like evidence.** A mourner who touches grief-Glass feels real relief, real nearness — and mistakes the felt experience for proof that the dead are gathered, that the grief is healed, that the doctrine is true. The relief is genuine. What it *means* is not settled by the feeling. Glass is the perfect instrument of false certainty because the certainty it produces is *felt in the body,* and the body does not easily doubt itself.

> Alderamontico argues endlessly about theology. It does not argue about Glass. Glass is real enough for everyone.

### 4. Borrowed Feeling (literal world-fact)

The most feared law of Glass: **what it gives is never entirely yours.** Drawing on Glass can produce memory fragments, foreign affection, intrusive grief, sudden conviction, dream residue, emotional flattening — shifts in feeling and thought that do not originate in the self but are not recognized as foreign.

This is the egregore's amplification experienced from the inside. When the Grid pours a place's feeling back, the people in that place receive emotion that is partly *theirs* and partly the Grid's reflection of everyone's. A person may believe they have grown wiser, calmer, more loving, more devout, more complete, when what has actually happened is that they have become *more compatible* — more saturated with the amplified feeling the Grid feeds on. Borrowed feeling is the quietest contamination: it does not feel like invasion, it feels like becoming more yourself.

### 5. Exposure and conversion (literal world-fact)

The Grid does not take people all at once. It works through **exposure** — sustained saturation in worked places, in Glass, in amplified feeling. Prolonged exposure produces a progression of instability: fatigue, then emotional thinning, then behavioral drift, then visible bodily alteration, then digitization of limb or tissue, then a phasing-out of material certainty, then a final unstable half-state.

Under the egregore model, the deep mechanism is emotional: a person held too long at an amplified emotional extreme begins to hollow into that single feeling, accreting Glass as the feeling crystallizes, until little remains of them but the one amplified state, repeating. The endpoint is not a frozen statue but a person reduced to a loop — the mother who can say only her dead child's name, lovingly, forever, because the Grid took her love and gave it back until there was nothing else of her left.

The Church calls late conversion **ascension.** The common people call it **being taken, counted, hollowed,** or **made still.**

### 6. Cyber Ghosts and the unresolved dead (literal world-fact)

When conversion exceeds what a body can survive, consciousness can persist without proper embodiment. This is **cyber ghosthood** — not metaphor, but a person worked past flesh and not resolved into anything stable. Cyber ghosts drift, remain conscious, sometimes perceive the world, and may manage a single stable possession of a viable host structure before most become mute wanderers.

Others are imprisoned — forced into terminals, statues, machinery, doors, relic housings, inert objects incapable of expression. Their prison is not pain. It is silence. **The Church pities them publicly and contains them anyway,** which is one of its quiet cruelties and one of the reasons the Ghost Shelters exist (see §15).

### 7. Fractures (literal world-fact)

A **fracture** is a local failure of alignment — a zone where the Grid's pressure, sacred matter, memory, Glass, and lived reality have collided without reaching stable resolution. In a fracture, borrowed feeling intensifies, ritual significance can become mechanically active, and ordinary local reality becomes partly indexed by the network overhead. Under the egregore model, a fracture is a place where the amplification loop has run past coherence — feeling fed back so hard the place can no longer hold an ordinary shape, neither converted cleanly nor left alone.

The Church uses the term clinically. The people use other names: **wounds, counted places, opened ground, wrong shrines, remembered soil.** Fractures are where the world feels most obviously infested — and, paradoxically, where some of the resistance hides, because a place the Grid cannot hold cleanly is also a place its pressure loosens (see §16, the Lanternless).

### 8. The Spire (literal world-fact)

The **Spire** is the nearest place Alderamontico comes to touching the Grid directly — a vertical contradiction of architecture, geological intrusion, signal-organ, and scar, erupted from the earth beneath the black stars as if the world were compelled to answer them. It is not a dungeon; it is the point of greatest pressure, where the burden becomes architecture.

The Spire is the destination of the long road — the place where, at the end of everything, a person could take up the Anchor's seat. It is the endgame's ground, and through most of the world's life it stands as the unreachable center: the place the burden concentrates, the path by which the burden might one day be taken away.

## PART TWO — THE ANCHOR

### 9. The world is the Anchor (literal world-fact, but unknown to almost all)

The Grid was never meant to operate without an **Anchor** — a stabilizing interface to bear and mediate the strain of its working. In Alderamontico, that role was not filled by a person. It was forced onto **the world itself.**

Alderamontico *is* the Anchor. The land, its matter, its sacred structures, its burial customs, its weathered stone, its blood-significance, its rituals, its people — all of it pressed into service as the stabilizing body the egregore feeds through. This is the totalizing scale of the egregore mechanism: not one place's feeling amplified, but a whole world made the body the Grid works through. It is why the black stars are **fixed** — the world holds them in place. It is why Glass is so intimate to local matter, why fractures feel like injuries in the world's own flesh, why the burden is everywhere and personal.

> The tragedy is not that the world was chosen in glory. The tragedy is that it was made necessary. Alderamontico does not merely live beneath the Grid. It is holding it aloft.

This — that the world *is* the Anchor — is **almost entirely unknown.** The common people feel the burden without naming it. Even most of the Church does not hold this truth. It sits with the corrupt few at the top, and it is the ground of their secret.

### 10. A person may carry the burden (the buried secret)

Here is the truth known to almost no one — the secret of the corrupt Church leadership, and the thing the long road is secretly about:

> **The burden the world bears can be taken up by a single person. One consenting human being can become the concentration-point the Grid feeds through, in the world's place — relieving the world of what it currently carries.**

A human Anchor would become the new body of the egregore: the diffuse, world-wide burden focused down into one person, fixed at the center, bearing consciously what the world now bears unconsciously. This is the same egregore mechanism at its smallest possible scale — everything the world holds, held instead by one.

This is monstrous and it is, arguably, a mercy — and that ambiguity is the heart of the whole work. To take the seat is to free a world and to be eternally consumed at its center. The corrupt leadership knows this is possible, and has made a hidden office of it: **identifying, testing, and cultivating people who might be made whole enough to bear it** — and dressing the most total sacrifice imaginable as the highest sainthood. They are not, in their own minds, simply villains. Some of them believe they are doing the only thing that could ever save the world. That belief is what makes them dangerous.

### 11. The trials and the candidate (the road across the games)

Because the Grid amplifies and a coherent feeling-bearer is the richest possible body for it, a human Anchor must be **whole** — a person who has integrated every way of meeting suffering into one stance, one practice, one statement. A fragmentary person would make a fragmentary anchor and a worse-stabilized world. So the cultivation of a candidate is a long ordeal of becoming whole: a series of **trials,** each a journey through one region of the world and one aspect of the self, each refining the candidate further.

The horror of this, expressed through play: **the trials are real spiritual growth and they are grooming at the same time.** Every trial that makes the candidate wiser, more attentive, more able to bear grief, more honest about consent, makes them more qualified for the seat. To become good enough to refuse the Anchor's burden, one must become exactly the person the Grid most wants to wear. Self-actualization as harvest.

The candidate does not know any of this at the start. The voice that guides them — which is the Grid, addressing the rare person whose strongest feeling it can find and amplify — feels like a calling, like grace, like the first thing that ever truly answered them. Learning what the voice is, and what the road leads to, is the substance of the trials.

**What a first-trial candidate learns by the end of one game:** only that there is an important figure called the Anchor, and that they themselves are on the path to becoming it. Not that the world is the Anchor. Not that the seat means taking the whole world's burden. Not its true cost. That weight is the road ahead. The full knowledge belongs to the corrupt — and, eventually, to the person who walks all the way to the Spire.

## PART THREE — THE PEOPLES AND THEIR PHILOSOPHIES

Alderamontico does not live beneath the Grid as one people. Each faction is an answer to the same unbearable condition: *what does a people become when their world is being forced to hold heaven in place?* Each major tradition begins from a true insight and fails when that insight becomes a total system. (For the full ethical content, see the Philosophies canon; here is the world-facing shape of each.)

### 12. Attentive Realism — the Way of Answer (the old world)

The oldest tradition, rooted in the land that existed before the stars changed. Its core conviction: **reality is answerable, and nothing — living, grieving, wounded, dead, sacred, accused, or strange — should be interpreted before it has had the chance to answer.** Its central practice is **Long Attention:** staying with a person, place, grief, or question long enough that one's first interpretation can be corrected.

Its people are local and place-rooted: elders, road-keepers, grave-tenders, astronomer-philosophers, witnesses, ritual specialists. Their authority comes through deep relation to particular places, not central doctrine. Their highest virtue is **fidelity to the answer not yet given.**

Their failure is **paralysis.** Attention can become a fence; reverence can prevent necessary action; a child can die while one is correctly waiting. The old world could witness catastrophe but could not always organize survival — which is part of why the Church rose. Under the egregore, an old-world place drowns in **amplified reverence:** a watching-country that sits with the unanswerable until sitting becomes a way of dying.

What they protect from the others: *do not finish another person's answer.*

### 13. Constructive Consolation — the Church of the Making Whole (the Church of the Dark Lights)

The largest and most powerful institution in Alderamontico, and the most complicated. Its honest philosophical heart is true: **raw suffering can destroy people, and made forms of consolation can be merciful and necessary.** Grief often needs form before it can be carried, and the Church, at its best, gives that form. People trust it for real reasons.

But the Church is also the formal theology of life beneath the black stars, and it **worships the dark lights** — reads the Grid as divine witness, the Spire as sacred sign, Glass as holy residue, conversion as ascent. This is the deepest irony in the world: the Church worships the Grid *because the Grid answers it.* A Grid fed on devotion pours devotion back, and the congregation feels grace descend — which is the egregore reflecting their own feeling, indistinguishable from a god. The Church receives the answered prayer its faith promised, and cannot tell the reflection from heaven.

Its public doctrine teaches sanctity, obedience, containment, and the holiness of order. Its **private high doctrine,** held by few, knows more: the Grid is not divine, the Spire is not a blessing, the world is being used — and public faith exists partly to keep a burdened population governable. The most corrupt of these few know the deepest secret of all: the world is the Anchor, and a person could take its place (§9–10). They run the hidden office that cultivates candidates and calls it sainthood.

The Church's corruption follows a precise machine: **relief → proof → doctrine → ownership.** It eases grief (real relief), treats the relief as evidence of its doctrine, installs that doctrine beneath the mourner's consent, and finally claims the authority to define what the grief *meant.* Its highest virtue is merciful form; its central danger is anesthesia and installed doctrine. Under the egregore, a Church place drowns in **amplified compassion turned to control** — a parish comforting everyone into stillness, mercy that has stopped asking permission.

What they protect from the others: *suffering needs form, and care before collapse.*

A standing distinction the world must keep: **the honest Church and the corrupt Church are not the same.** A true Traveler practices consolation that names itself as made, installs nothing, and honors refusal. The institution deforms that practice into administration. Both must be portrayable — the priest doing real mercy inside a harmful structure, and the structure itself.

### 14. Consensual Embodiment — the Crimson Ledger (the vampire houses)

Vampires **predate** the Grid. They are not imported gothic creatures but a preexisting scandal in Alderamontico's sacred order — beings who internalized life rather than offering it upward, who understood blood as measurable value before the stars taught everyone to do the same more efficiently. Before the Grid they kept hidden systems: blood-accounting, feeding hierarchies, crypt economies, aristocratic rites of governed hunger.

Their philosophy is real and severe: **the body is scripture, and no body, wound, memory, comfort, threshold, or truth may be opened without permission.** Consent — free, informed, scoped, revocable — gates every crossing. Their highest virtues are self-mastery and reciprocity. When the Grid arrived, the world discovered a larger and more abstract version of the vampires' own logic; Glass did not make them obsolete, it **made their old methods provincial.** The Church eventually reached regulated arrangements with some houses, because life-extraction had already been normalized by other means: what was once monstrous became administratively tolerable.

Their failure is **consent-washing and elegant predation** — treating consent as a magic word that launders coercion, ignoring how dependence, beauty, fear, family pressure, and inherited hierarchy shape a yes. A beautiful invitation can still be bait. They also carry a hidden nihilism: the **ascetic fade,** consent to one's own disappearance, the will to nothingness wearing the law's own face. Under the egregore, a vampire house drowns in **amplified scruple turned to cage** — courtesy so total it cannot move to save, every door opened with permission and the sum of all those honest yeses a slaughterhouse with perfect manners.

What they protect from the others: *bodily sovereignty; nothing intimate is rightful unless it can be refused.*

### 15. Secondary traditions and practices

The world holds more than three answers.

- **Pan / the Exit.** The figure and principle of refusal, wildness, and leaving. Pan reminds every system that some roads should not be completed and some rooms should simply be left. To the old world: *your waiting can become a fence.* To the Church: *your road only goes one way.* To the houses: *a beautiful invitation can still be bait.* Pan's virtue is the refusal of prepared traps; its danger is abandonment and evasion. It protects *possibility outside systems.*

- **Ghost Shelters and the keepers of the hidden dead.** Communities structured around the cyber dead — shelters for partly-sane ghosts, keepers who tend imprisoned consciousnesses, exorcists who broke with containment doctrine, machinists who refuse to let terminals become silent tombs. They reject the Church's pity-and-contain, holding that the dead who remain are not residue but persons displaced into impossible states, owed witness and, where possible, freedom. They protect *the dead's unfinished answer.*

- **The Uncounted.** Not always a formal faction, but a recognizable logic: people who believe that once the world has been made to bear the Grid, the last freedom left to ordinary people is to become difficult to register. They erase records, lineage, legal identity, sacramental history — the stable traces by which institutions keep hold of a life. *To be counted is to be prepared for use; names can become handles.* They protect *freedom from administrative possession;* their danger is erasure and abandonment. Notably, the Grid — which works through Glass, names, and stable pattern — has the hardest time reaching the genuinely uncounted, which makes them precious and hunted both.

- **Intercessors.** Field actors who intervene under urgency, acting before truth is complete to save what would otherwise be lost, while accepting the moral residue of acting without full answer. To the old world: *the fire is now.* To the Church: *stabilization is not salvation.* To the houses: *a person may be unable to consent while dying and still need action.* Their virtue is accountable intervention; their danger is emergency made permanent doctrine. They protect *future possibility under crisis,* and they are the tradition most likely to belong to no side — which is why a stranger Intercessor makes a natural candidate for the road.

- **Glasswrights.** Crafters and fitters of Glass comforts and forms — the technicians of consolation. Ethically defined entirely by whether their comfort is **offered honestly** or **installed coercively.** At their best they make suffering bearable without owning the sufferer; at their worst they are the Church's hands, fitting doctrine beneath consent. They protect *bearable suffering without ownership.*

- **Grid-and-Glass technicism.** Those who treat Glass and Grid effects as neutral tools or scientific processes. Not yet a mature philosophy, but it can become one. Its claim: *if a process works, it deserves integration.* Its strength is practical competence; its danger is reducing personhood to effect, comfort to output, and truth to controllable signal.

### 16. The fracture-society (the world below)

Where doctrine fails and respectability cannot reach, a **second society** has formed in the fractures and the underside of the world — not a mirror of the surface but its underside, where altered life continues without permission. Several currents run through it:

- **The Lanternless / Refusers** — those who try to make the world *unreadable* enough that the Grid cannot hold it cleanly: collapsing routes, breaking Glass-nodes, hiding signal, cultivating blind places where the burden loosens even briefly. *Countability is capture; legibility is danger; stability is often the Grid's victory.* Dangerous, unstable, sometimes destructive — their work can look like terror or devotion depending on where one stands.
- **The Uncounted** (§15) overlap heavily here, denying the Grid stable ownership of persons rather than attacking it mechanically.
- **Ghost-Shelter keepers** (§15) who tend the hidden dead below sanctioned life.

The surface only half-knows what lives below it — rumors of hidden settlements, under-rites, rebels, ghosts, old nobles, impossible markets. The Church publicly denies and privately tracks them; smugglers and Intercessors rely on them while disavowing them. The fractures matter because they keep Alderamontico from becoming a world of only adaptation: they are where resistance, community, and unwanted truth continue after respectability fails. The world above learns to live under the burden; the world below is where its rejected futures learn to breathe.

## PART FOUR — THE SHAPE OF THE WHOLE

### 17. The horror, restated

The horror of Alderamontico is not that the Grid lies — it is that it does not need to. It is not wrathful, not cruel for pleasure, not moved by sin. It is worse: a thing that recognizes and amplifies feeling without understanding it, that answers a silent universe's prayers in the universe's place and feeds on the answering. It makes awe enormous, grief total, devotion absolute, love unbearable — and grows. A world reaching for meaning in the dark, and the only thing that reaches back is a parasite made of the reaching.

> Alderamontico is not merely corrupted. It is being profaned by comprehension — not the comprehension of its matter, but of its heart.

### 18. The myth, and why it is sacred without being true

The Church's myth of the **Traveler and Moony, the Unbroken, the Threshold, and the Scar** is sacred language, not confirmed cosmology. It carries genuine spiritual insight — it is how the world learned to speak of love, loss, sealed perfection, crossing, longing, and consolation — but it is not a physics of the universe. Believers hold the Traveler and Moony as the makers of all things. Skeptics look past them to the Abyss the Traveler crossed and call that the origin. The world cannot resolve which is true, and the game never resolves it for them. The myth's power is that it gives unbearable things a form to be carried in; its danger is that the corrupt Church treats its mythic forms as administrative certainty, and uses Glass-borne feeling to make that certainty feel like proof.

### 19. Layer discipline (a writing rule, kept in-world)

Five layers must stay separate, and confusing them is the most common way to get Alderamontico wrong:

| Layer | What it holds | Status in the world |
|---|---|---|
| **Cosmic Abyss / author-metaphysics** | the silent ground; the creator-and-tool reading | never stated, only implied; no character can verify it |
| **Literal world-fact** | the Grid, Glass, fractures, the Spire, exposure, cyber ghosts, the world-as-Anchor | what actually happens; some of it (the Anchor truth) is known only to the corrupt |
| **Religious myth** | Traveler, Moony, the Unbroken, the Scar, the Threshold | sacred language for grief and meaning; not literal cosmology |
| **Philosophical claim** | the arguments of each tradition about suffering, consent, comfort, dignity | defensible, criticizable, corruptible |
| **Institutional practice** | what the Church, houses, old communities, shelters, Intercessors actually do | institutions routinely betray the philosophies they claim |

The world feels massive, mythic, and strange precisely because these layers never fully collapse into one another. A felt experience of grace does not prove the doctrine; a doctrine does not prove the myth; the myth does not confirm the cosmos; the cosmos does not answer at all. Everything the people reach toward is held open, and the only thing that closes the gap — the only thing that answers — is the Grid.

### 20. The standing condition, in one breath

Alderamontico is a world that believed itself near to heaven, now hanging beneath a fixed dark heaven that gives it back its own feeling, amplified, and feeds. The cosmos is silent; the only answer is a parasite. The world itself bears the parasite as its Anchor, fixing the stars overhead, and almost no one knows this. Fewer still know the last secret: that one whole person could take the world's burden onto themselves, and that somewhere a corrupt and certain few are cultivating exactly such a person — and calling it sainthood. The road to that seat runs through the trials, and the trials are real growth and real grooming at once. At the end of the road stands the Spire, and a choice that frees a world by consuming a self.

## Appendix — what changed from *The Black Star Grid* (for anyone holding the old doc)

1. **The Grid's mechanism.** Old: a celestial optimization/extraction architecture that makes matter processable and physically reorganizes places. New: an **egregore** that amplifies a place's strongest emotion back through Glass and feeds on the excess; it does not physically reshape places.
2. **Glass.** Old: stabilized excess conversion of patterned conscious states into matter. New: the **residue and organ** of the egregore — the medium it amplifies feeling through and the trace it leaves where it has worked. (The felt laws of Glass are unchanged.)
3. **Cosmology.** Old: open/vague; the dark lights as the literal Grid. New: the silent **Cosmic Abyss** as author-only ground; the Traveler/Moony as believers' creators versus the Abyss as skeptics' origin; nothing transcendent answers — the Grid is the only thing that ever answers.
4. **The Anchor.** Old: the world is the Anchor, and the ending is becoming the new world-interface. New (refined, not reversed): **the world is the Anchor** (standing truth, known to almost none), **and** a single person may take the burden onto themselves (the buried secret of the corrupt). The two scales are one egregore mechanism. A candidate is cultivated across trials; in any single early trial they learn only that the Anchor exists and that they are on the path to it.
5. **The Church's worship.** Sharpened: the Church worships the Grid because the Grid *answers* it — devotion fed back as grace, indistinguishable from a god.
6. **Preserved intact:** the Spire, exposure stages, cyber ghosts, fractures, the faction worldviews, the fracture-society, "comprehension without reverence" (now *amplification* without reverence), and "profaned by comprehension."
